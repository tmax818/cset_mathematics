4. calculus

4.1. calculus

4.1.1. trigonometry

  1. prove that the Pythagorean Theorem is equivalent to the trigonimic identity \(sin^2\)
    1. pythagorean theorem
  2. prove and apply the sine, cosine, and tangent sum formulae for all real values
    1. sum formulas
      1. applying the sum formulas
  3. analyze properties of trigonometric functions in a variety of ways(e.g. graphing and solving problems using the unit circle).
    1. the unit circle definiton
  4. apply the definitions and properties of inverse trigonometric functions(i.e., arcsine, arccos, and arctan)

4.1.2. limits and continuity

  1. derive basic properties of limits and continuity, including the sum, difference, product, constant multiple, and quotient rules using the formal definition of a limit.
    1. limits
      1. limit theorems
      2. one-sided limits
      3. limits involving infinity
      4. other facts about limits
    2. continuity
  2. show that a polynomial function is continuous at a point
  3. apply the intermediate value theorem, using the geometric implications of continutiy
    1. the intermediate value theorem

4.1.3. derivatives and applications

  1. derive the rules of differentiation for polynomials, trigonometric, logrithmic functions using the formal definition of derivative
    1. rules of differentiation
    2. chain rule
    3. implicit differentiation
    4. derivatives of exponents
    5. dervatives of logarithms
    6. derviatives of trigonometric functions
  2. interpret the concept of derivative geometrically, numerically and analytically(i.e. slope of a tangent, limit of a difference quotient, extrema, Newton's Method and instantaneous rate of change).
    1. extrema
    2. Newton's Method
  3. interpret both continuous and differentiable functions geometrically and analytically and apply Rolle's Theorem, the mean value theorem, and L'Hopital's Rule
    1. continuous and differentiable functions
      1. why continuity and differentiability matter
    2. consequences of the mean value theorem
    3. [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%27H%C3%B4pital%27s_rule][L'Hopital's Rule

      ]]

    4. other indeterminate forms
  4. use the dervative to solve rectilinear motion, related rate, and optimization problems
    1. antiderivatives
      1. some basic antiderivatives
  5. use the derivative to analyze functions and planar curves (e.g. maxima, minima, inflection points, concavity)
  6. solve separable first-order differential equations and apply them to growth and decay problems

4.1.4. integrals and applications

  1. derive definite integrals of standard algebraic functions using the formal definition of the integral
  2. interpret the concept of a definite integral geometrically, numerically, and analytically(e.g., limit of Riemann sums)
  3. prove the fundamental theorem of calculus, and use it to interpret definite integrals as antiderivatives.
  4. apply the concept of integrals to compute the length of a curve and the areas and volumes of geometric figures.

4.1.5. sequences and series

  1. derive and apply the formulae for the sums of finite arithmetic series and finite geometric series(e.g. express repeating decimals as a rational number)
  2. determine convergence of a given sequence or series using standard techniques(e.g. ratio, comparison, integral tests)
  3. calculate Taylor series and Taylor polynomials of basic functions
